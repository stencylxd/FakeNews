Beware of Fake News!

In the online world, opinions are everywhere. You have to make sure you are getting facts, not opinions
when looking online. Websites like Twitter or Facebook are very vulnerable to this becuase you can pay
money to advertise FAKE news! Have you seen this kind of stuff floating around? Well here are some
ways that you can detect fake news!

Does it make sense to me?

If it's something not even close to common sense, such as "PURPLE DEER ARE KILLING OFF THE POPULATION"
then it's probably fake. You can also connect dots by comparing other news sources to see if they have the same
story. If lots of news sources are telling the same things, then the stories are probably fact. If there is only one story,
then it's probably not true.

Is this a trusted news source?

Have you been using this news source before? Does it credit outside sources and use pictures/audio/video? If so,
you are probably looking at a true story. But make sure you check the outside soruces first. People will put in a lot
of effort into tricking you. The news source in question and the credited sources could all be made by the same guy.
One other thing to take into account is whether the other stories the news source created make sense. If they only
have one story, you probably shouldn't trust the source. If the other stories don't make sense, then you probably shouldn't
trust the source.

Conclusion

Fake news is everywhere, and common online. If you want a reliable news source, you should read the local newspaper.
It's very easy to setup a real looking news website, so you should always be careful when reading a story or article.
If a story doesn't make sense and doesn't have external sources or media (sound, video, pictures) then it's probably
false. You should also check other news sources to see if they are talking about the same thing, which can backup
that story and source. If you haven't heard the news source from anywhere (communinty, friends, online reviews), then
you probably should do some reasearch on it. Some sources I recommend are: CNN, NPR, and BBC.
